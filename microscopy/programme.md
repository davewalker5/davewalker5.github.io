# Programme of Microscopic Investigations

## Series 0 — Basic Discipline with the Instrument

| No. | Investigation                         | Example Specimens                     | Aim                                                     |
| --- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0.1 | Orientation of the Microscopic Field  | Stage micrometer                      | Understand image inversion and field orientation.       |
| 0.2 | Regulation of Illumination            | Prepared slide (any specimen)         | Learn correct condenser focus and illumination control. |
| 0.3 | Comparison of Magnifying Powers       | Stage micrometer                      | Compare field size and detail at different objectives.  |
| 0.4 | Calibration of the Eyepiece Graticule | Stage micrometer                      | Establish measurement capability.                       |
| 0.5 | Observation of Limits of Resolution   | Diatom test slide, fine fabric fibres | Observe smallest visible detail under high power.       |
| 0.6 | Recording Observations                | Any specimen previously examined      | Practice diagrammatic recording and labelling.          |

## Series I — Surface & Exchange

| No. | Investigation                               | Example Specimens                                    | Aim                                            |
| --- | ------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| I.1 | Examination of the Epidermis of a Leaf      | Ivy (Hedera helix), privet, laurel                   | Observe epidermal cells and surface pattern.   |
| I.2 | Observation of Stomata                      | Spider plant (Chlorophytum), tradescantia, lily leaf | Study stomatal structure and gas exchange.     |
| I.3 | Examination of Plant Hairs (Trichomes)      | Tomato leaf, geranium, nettle                        | Observe protective and sensory plant hairs.    |
| I.4 | Examination of the Wing of an Insect        | Housefly, crane fly, moth wing                       | Observe membrane structure and veins.          |
| I.5 | Observation of the Insect Cuticle           | Beetle leg, ant, small fly                           | Study segmentation and protective exoskeleton. |
| I.6 | Examination of the Surface of Pollen Grains | Lily, daisy, dandelion                               | Observe sculptured pollen surfaces.            |

## Series II — Reproductive Elements

| No.  | Investigation                               | Example Specimens                            | Aim                                 |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| II.1 | Examination of Pollen from Flowering Plants | Lily, tulip, daisy                           | Observe pollen grain structure.     |
| II.2 | Comparison of Pollen from Different Species | Grass, rose, pine pollen                     | Compare pollen morphology.          |
| II.3 | Observation of Fern Spores                  | Bracken (Pteridium), garden ferns            | Examine sporangia and spores.       |
| II.4 | Observation of Moss Capsules                | Common moss (Bryum, Funaria)                 | Study capsule and spore production. |
| II.5 | Examination of Fungal Spores                | Bread mould (Rhizopus), penicillium on fruit | Observe fungal reproduction.        |

## Series III — Support & Conduction

| No.   | Investigation                       | Example Specimens                     | Aim                              |
| ----- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| III.1 | Cross-Section of an Herbaceous Stem | Ivy stem, sunflower stem              | Identify vascular bundles.       |
| III.2 | Cross-Section of a Woody Stem       | Young twig of ash, elder, or hawthorn | Observe early woody tissue.      |
| III.3 | Examination of Leaf Venation        | Ivy leaf, holly leaf                  | Observe vascular network.        |
| III.4 | Observation of Plant Fibres         | Celery stalk, flax fibre              | Examine elongated support cells. |
| III.5 | Examination of Root Structure       | Onion root, young seedling roots      | Observe root tissues.            |

## Series IV — Aquatic Microscopic Life

| No.  | Investigation                       | Example Specimens                     | Aim                                   |
| ---- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| IV.1 | Survey of Pond Water                | Pond, ditch, rainwater barrel         | Identify diverse microorganisms.      |
| IV.2 | Examination of Freshwater Algae     | Filamentous algae from pond edge      | Observe algal cell chains.            |
| IV.3 | Observation of Diatoms              | Pond water, wet stone scrapings       | Study silica frustules.               |
| IV.4 | Observation of Protozoa             | Stagnant pond sample                  | Observe locomotion and feeding.       |
| IV.5 | Observation of Rotifers             | Pond water with plant debris          | Examine complex microscopic animals.  |
| IV.6 | Observation of Pond Micro-Ecosystem | Mature pond sample after several days | Observe interactions among organisms. |

## Series V — Bud Structure

| No. | Investigation                    | Example Specimens                    | Aim                                 |
| --- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------- |
| V.1 | Examination of Bud Scales        | Horse chestnut, beech                | Observe protective bud coverings.   |
| V.2 | Section of a Dormant Bud         | Ivy bud, hawthorn bud                | Examine embryonic leaves.           |
| V.3 | Examination of a Developing Leaf | Opening bud of sycamore or rose      | Study leaf differentiation.         |
| V.4 | Observation of the Shoot Apex    | Young shoot tip of ivy or bean plant | Observe meristematic growth tissue. |

## Series VI — Decay & Biological Recycling

| No.  | Investigation                          | Example Specimens                   | Aim                                        |
| ---- | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| VI.1 | Observation of Mould Growth            | Bread mould, mouldy fruit           | Observe fungal hyphae.                     |
| VI.2 | Examination of Decaying Leaf Tissue    | Fallen leaf in compost or damp soil | Observe breakdown of plant cells.          |
| VI.3 | Observation of Fungal Spore Structures | Mould growing on citrus peel        | Study spore formation.                     |
| VI.4 | Examination of Microbial Films         | Slimy layer on standing water       | Observe microbial communities.             |
| VI.5 | Observation of Soil Micro-Organisms    | Garden soil suspended in water      | Study organisms involved in decomposition. |
