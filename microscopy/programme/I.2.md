## Aim
To study the structure and distribution of stomata in a leaf epidermis.

## Specimen & Apparatus
- Fresh leaf (e.g. spider plant, tradescantia, or lily)
- Forceps or needle
- Slide and coverslip
- Water
- Compound microscope

## Method
1. Prepare an epidermal peel from the lower surface of the leaf.
2. Mount the peel in a drop of water on a slide.
3. Apply a coverslip carefully.
4. Focus under low power and locate stomata.
5. Increase magnification for detailed examination.

## Observations to Make
- Shape and arrangement of guard cells.
- Form of the stomatal aperture.
- Distribution of stomata across the surface.
- Differences between surrounding epidermal cells and guard cells.
