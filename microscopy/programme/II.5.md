## Aim
To observe the structure and distribution of fungal spores.

## Specimen & Apparatus
- Fungal growth (e.g. bread mould or penicillium on fruit)
- Needle or mounted pin
- Slide and coverslip
- Water
- Compound microscope

## Method
1. Using a needle, collect a very small portion of the fungal material.
2. Transfer to a slide and add a drop of water.
3. Gently tease the material to separate structures.
4. Apply a coverslip carefully.
5. Examine under low power, then increase magnification.

## Observations to Make
- Presence and arrangement of spores.
- Shape and size of individual spores.
- Relationship between spores and supporting structures.
- Any variation within the sample.
