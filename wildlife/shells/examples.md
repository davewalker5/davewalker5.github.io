# The Example Shell Presets

The examples presented here, and included as presets with the shell generator, explore a small region of shell morphospace generated by the computational growth model described in the preceding chapters.

Each shell begins with the same underlying principles: logarithmic growth, an evolving aperture and the accumulation of shell material through time. By altering growth trajectory, aperture geometry, coiling behaviour and ornamentation, a diverse range of shell forms emerges from a common developmental framework.

The resulting models are intentionally simplified, but they demonstrate how recognisable shell architectures can arise from surprisingly small changes in growth rules. Rather than representing particular species, the presets can be viewed as experiments in computational morphology: explorations of the relationship between growth process and form.
