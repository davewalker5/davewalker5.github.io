# Annulated Orthocone-Like Shell

{% assign assets_path = site.assets_url | append: "/images/modelling/shells/" %}
{% include image-block.html images=page.images assets=assets_path %}

The annulated orthocone extends the orthocone shell model by introducing discrete transverse growth rings, or annulations, along the shell surface.

Many orthoconic cephalopods possessed ornamentation that differed markedly from the fine ribs often seen in coiled shells. Instead of continuous ribbing, the shell surface was divided into a series of prominent growth increments that produced a segmented appearance along the shell axis.

Within the shell morphology framework, the annulated orthocone provided an opportunity to explore a different class of shell ornamentation and highlighted an important distinction between continuous surface ornament and discrete growth structures.

## From Ribbing to Annulations

The original ribbing system was designed primarily for spiral shells such as ammonites and gastropods.

In those forms, ornamentation can often be represented using a smooth periodic function applied continuously throughout growth. This produces regular ribs that follow the shell surface and vary smoothly in amplitude.

Applied to an orthocone, however, the same approach produced a shell that resembled a corrugated tube rather than a segmented cephalopod shell.

The annulated orthocone therefore introduced a second ornamentation strategy. Rather than applying a continuous sinusoidal displacement, ornamentation is generated as a series of discrete growth bands separated by relatively smooth shell segments.

This produces a shell that reads as a sequence of growth increments rather than a continuously ribbed surface.

## Ornamentation as Growth Structure

The annulated orthocone revealed an important distinction within the modelling framework.

The original ribbing system represents continuous surface ornamentation:

- Fine ribs
- Growth lines
- Repeating shell sculpture

The annulation system instead represents discrete developmental events:

- Major growth increments
- Shell segmentation
- Episodic shell thickening

Although both are generated by modifying the shell surface, they correspond to different biological interpretations and produce visually distinct shell architectures.

This suggests that shell ornamentation may be viewed as comprising at least two broad classes:

- Continuous ornamentation
- Discrete growth ornamentation

The annulated orthocone provided the first implementation of the latter.

## Relationship to Chamber Structure

An interesting question raised during development concerned the relationship between external annulations and internal chambers.

In real cephalopods, chamber formation and external shell ornamentation are not necessarily coupled. Chamber spacing and growth ornament may arise from different biological processes and need not occur at the same intervals.

However, initial models in which annulations and chamber septa were generated independently produced visual rhythms that appeared unrelated.

The final implementation therefore links major annulations to chamber spacing. This creates a correspondence between external shell segmentation and the underlying chamber architecture, producing a shell whose internal and external structures appear developmentally related.

While not intended as a strict biological reconstruction, this approach generates a coherent and easily interpretable shell morphology.

## Modelling Observations

The annulated orthocone demonstrated that relatively small changes in ornamentation can produce substantial changes in perceived morphology.

Unlike the transition from logarithmic spirals to orthocones, which required a different growth trajectory, the annulated orthocone uses the same underlying shell architecture as the standard orthocone. The growth axis, aperture expansion, chamber generation and siphuncle construction remain unchanged.

Only the ornamentation strategy differs.

Despite this, the resulting shell possesses a noticeably different visual character and resembles a distinct class of cephalopod shell.

This reinforces a broader theme of the project: shell morphology emerges not only from growth trajectory and aperture geometry, but also from the interaction between developmental structure and surface ornamentation.

The annulated orthocone therefore represents both a new shell form and the introduction of a new ornamentation family within the computational shell morphology framework.
